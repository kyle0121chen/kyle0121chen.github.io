Hi, I'm Min-Yue Chen

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at Michigan State University, where I work in the Security, Networking, and Mobile Systems Research Lab. My research sits at the intersection of mobile systems, cellular security, networked systems, and AI for systems.

I am a wireless networking researcher and systems engineer focused on understanding how real-world infrastructure breaks under adversarial conditions and on building practical, standards-compliant defenses. My work combines standards-driven analysis, end-to-end security testing, and mobile systems research to identify security weaknesses and validate deployable mitigations. I build experimental platforms based on SDR-based 4G and 5G testbeds for rigorous evaluation, reproducible validation, and practical defense design across mobile and wireless systems.

Selected Publications

ACM MobiSys 2026

Insecurity of Lost/Stolen Phone Reporting Services: Vulnerabilities, Attacks, and Countermeasures

Min-Yue Chen, Y. Hu, Y.-A. Chen, C.-Y. Li, T. Xie, G.-H. Tu

Accepted by Proceedings of ACM the 24th ACM International Conference on Mobile Systems, Applications, and Services (MobiSys) 2026, June 21 - 25, 2026, Cambridge, UK (Acceptance rate: 68/302 = 22.51%).

  • Demonstrated how lost/stolen reporting services can be exploited to launch remote and silent cellular DoS attacks against arbitrary users and devices (e.g., smartphones, tablets, IoT)
  • Discovered 6 flaws across device and network domains by auditing 3GPP/GSMA protocols and analyzing the behavior of commercial reporting services on major U.S. carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile)
  • Built an end-to-end 5G SDR testbed from scratch (C/C++, srsRAN, free5GC) to develop and validate proposed standard-compliant security patches
ACM MobiCom 2022

Uncovering Insecure Designs of Cellular Emergency Services (911)

Y. Hu*, Min-Yue Chen*, G.-H. Tu, C.-Y. Li, S. Wang, J. Shi, T. Xie, L. Xiao, C. Peng, Z. Tan, and S. Lu

(*): Co-primary author, denotes equal contribution

Accepted by Proceedings of ACM the 28th Annual International Conference On Mobile Computing And Networking (MobiCom) 2022, October 17- October 21, 2022, Sydney, Australia (Acceptance rate: 56/314 = 17.83%).

Best Community Paper Award - Runner Up ACM SIGMOBILE Research Highlights AT&T Security Award
  • Uncovered 4 critical design flaws in cellular emergency services enabling anonymous users to obtain free, highest-priority data access and disrupt legitimate emergency services. Mitigations were subsequently adopted by AT&T
  • Built an SDR-based 4G/5G network testbed and engineered a custom emergency UE atop srsUE (C/C++) to benchmark topology resilience
  • Developed an Android testing toolkit (Emerg-Blocker), demonstrating hands-on experience with SIP/IMS, RRC procedures, and Qualcomm modem diagnostic trace analysis
ACM MobiCom 2024

Uncovering Problematic Designs Hindering Ubiquitous Cellular Emergency Services Access

Y. Hu, Min-Yue Chen, H. Yan, C.-Y. Cheng, G.-H. Tu, C.-Y. Li, T. Xie, C. Peng, L. Xiao, and J. Tang

Accepted by Proceedings of ACM the 30th Annual International Conference On Mobile Computing And Networking (MobiCom) 2024, September 30- October 4, 2024, Washington, D.C., USA (Acceptance rate: 55/288 = 19.09%, Winter run).

  • Uncovered that even with sufficient wireless signal coverage, users can still experience prolonged emergency call setup delays, call initiation failures, and unexpected call drops
  • Applied model-checking techniques to audit cellular emergency services in dynamic mobility scenarios, uncovering 11 counterintuitive logic flaws in 4G/5G designs
  • Architected an Android application utilizing Telephony APIs for dynamic PLMN selection and VoWiFi-based routing
