Hi, I'm Min-Yue Chen

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at Michigan State University, where I work in the Security, Networking, and Mobile Systems Research Lab. My research sits at the intersection of mobile systems, cellular security, networked systems, and AI for systems.

I focus on uncovering how real-world infrastructure fails under adversarial conditions and building practical defenses. I combine standard-driven analysis, end-to-end security testing, Android systems work, and SDR-based 4G/5G testbeds to validate deployable mitigations.

Research Experience

Insecurity of Lost/Stolen Phone Reporting Services

Jan. 2024 - Feb. 2026

  • Demonstrated how lost/stolen reporting services can be exploited to launch remote and silent cellular DoS attacks against arbitrary users and devices (e.g., smartphones, tablets, IoT).
  • Discovered 6 flaws across device and network domains by auditing 3GPP/GSMA protocols and analyzing the behavior of commercial reporting services on major U.S. carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile).
  • Built an end-to-end 5G SDR testbed from scratch (C/C++, srsRAN, free5GC) to develop and validate proposed standard-compliant security patches.

Insecure Designs of Cellular Emergency Services

Aug. 2021 - Dec. 2023

  • Uncovered 4 critical design flaws in cellular emergency services enabling anonymous users to obtain free, highest-priority data access and disrupt legitimate emergency services. Mitigations were subsequently adopted by AT&T.
  • Built an SDR-based 4G/5G network testbed and engineered a custom emergency UE atop srsUE (C/C++) to benchmark topology resilience.
  • Developed an Android testing toolkit (Emerg-Blocker), demonstrating hands-on experience with SIP/IMS, RRC procedures, and Qualcomm modem diagnostic trace analysis.

M911-Verifier: Emerg-specific Model Checking Tool

Dec. 2023 - Jun. 2024

  • Applied model-checking techniques to audit cellular emergency services in dynamic mobility scenarios, uncovering 11 counterintuitive logic flaws in 4G/5G designs.
  • Architected an Android application utilizing Telephony APIs for dynamic PLMN selection and VoWiFi-based routing.

Mobile Operating System Security

Aug. 2023 - Mar. 2025

  • Bypassed network policies by exploiting 4 vulnerabilities across baseband architectures, the Android Linux kernel, and IMS signaling routing to execute traffic hijacking, recognized as high-severity by Google Bug Bounty.
  • Prototyped a secure 5G architecture on an SDR testbed (srsRAN, open5GS) by modifying core network functions to integrate DHKE cryptography and MAC-based authentication.
